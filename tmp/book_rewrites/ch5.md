By 03:42 they were in a self-storage corridor in Croydon that smelled of wet concrete and dust.

Jonathan had chosen the hour because foot traffic dropped and because anyone following them would have to choose between obvious speed and obvious patience. Neither choice was invisible.

They arrived in sequence, never together: Peter first as lookout near the office vending machines, Diana second through the rear service gate, Aisha and Emma from opposite stairwells, Jonathan last with bolt cutters he hoped he would not need.

Unit C-119 sat halfway down the corridor.

The new lock glinted under strip lighting.

"No management tag," Aisha said quietly. "Whoever swapped it didn't want paper trails."

Jonathan crouched, touched the lock body once, then pulled his hand back.

"Warm."

Peter's expression sharpened. "Recently handled."

Diana swept the corridor with a compact RF detector.

"No active transmitters within ten meters," she said. "That does not mean no cameras."

Emma took a slow breath.

"We don't open blind."

Jonathan stood.

"If keys are still inside, we need in."

"If keys are gone, opening confirms we came," Aisha replied.

Peter checked the mirror fixed over the far fire door.

"We were confirmed the second we walked into this building."

He was right. At the end of the corridor, a red LED camera blinked above a signage panel that read _For your security_.

Emma looked at Jonathan.

"Open it. Fast and clean."

Jonathan knelt, set the cutters, and snapped the shackle in one controlled movement.

No alarms. No footsteps.

He rolled the shutter up just enough to slide under. Emma followed with her phone torch cupped, beam narrowed.

Inside: archive boxes, old duffel bags, a locked document case.

And empty foam where a hard drive case used to sit.

Jonathan froze.

"No."

He dropped to one knee, checking behind boxes, under tarp rolls, inside a crate marked _tax records_.

Nothing.

Aisha entered and scanned quickly.

"Search for what they missed, not what they took."

Peter stayed at the door, watching the corridor.

"Two minutes," he said.

Emma found a torn courier envelope under shelving, wedged near the wall. A printed routing sticker remained partially intact:

`BPL SECURE TRANSIT`

`PRIORITY: BLACK`

She held it up.

"Blackthorne," Peter said from the door.

Diana stepped in and lifted the locked document case.

"This is untouched."

Jonathan looked up.

"It shouldn't be."

"Unless they wanted this to remain," Diana said.

He stared at the case for one long second, then shook his head.

"I never used this for key material."

"Then open it anyway," Emma said.

Jonathan keyed the combination. The case clicked.

Inside: bank printouts, two passports under different names, and one sealed anti-static pouch containing a microSD card.

Aisha frowned.

"Not your decryption keys."

"No," Jonathan said. "Worse."

He flipped one passport open.

His photograph. Different name. Different birth date. Entry stamps from three countries he'd never visited.

Peter gave a low whistle.

"Continuity artifacts."

Diana nodded.

"Identity replacement kit."

Emma took the microSD pouch and turned it in her fingers.

Handwritten on masking tape:

`MONARCH / fallback`

She looked at Jonathan.

"Did you write this?"

"No."

"Could Marsh have?"

"No." He swallowed. "Her handwriting is tighter."

Peter's head snapped toward the corridor.

"Footsteps. Two sets."

He killed his torch.

Silence flooded the unit.

The footsteps stopped outside C-119.

Someone rattled the half-cut lock hanging loose from the shutter, once, almost politely.

Then a voice through the metal:

"Storage management. You need to leave this area immediately."

Aisha mouthed, _No badge check. No names._

Emma raised three fingers. On one, they moved.

Diana slipped out first through a narrow side clearance near the partition wall that led to a maintenance corridor she'd spotted on entry. Peter followed. Aisha next. Jonathan last after lowering the shutter enough to obscure the open unit.

Emma paused only long enough to tear the courier sticker free and pocket it.

They moved single file through concrete service passages until they hit an emergency exit that spilled into rain behind a tyre depot.

No one spoke until they were two blocks away under a railway bridge.

Jonathan leaned against wet brick, breathing hard.

"They got the drive."

Emma held up the microSD pouch.

"Maybe they did. Maybe they wanted us to take this instead."

Peter wiped rain from his face.

"Poisoned evidence?"

"Or breadcrumb," Diana said.

Aisha looked at the five of them in the sodium light, exhausted and shivering and very alive.

"Either way," she said, "we stop reacting."

Emma nodded.

"Agreed. Next location: offline machine, no network cards, no cloud backups. We inspect this card as hostile media."

Jonathan straightened, voice steadier now.

"If it's genuine MONARCH fallback data, we may have our first hard proof."

Peter looked back toward the dark line of warehouses.

"And if it's bait, they just taught us exactly how they want us to die."

Emma zipped the pouch into an inner pocket.

"Then we disappoint them."

At 04:11 her burner vibrated.

Unknown sender again.

`Good recovery.`

`See you at session.`
